Lets start with some of our most common flowers in fields:

On our open fields we have chicory which is a pretty common flower..

The exciting thing about Chicory is that you can eat it! The leaves are high in vitamins and minerals.

You can eat the leaves as a vegetable or in a salad, but beware, they are very bitter tasting.

The roots can also be boiled and eaten with butter. Sometimes the root is roasted and ground as a substitute or additive to coffee.

We also have forget me not.

The Forget-me-not is also known as the Scorpion Weed. The reason for this is because it has a coiled flower stalk like a tail of a scorpion.

Some also say the name Forget-me-not comes from this plant’s unpleasant taste or odor, which is hard to forget.

Next up we have Bee Balm.

When this NATIVE flower is in full bloom, birds and insects can’t resist visiting the nectar-rich flowers.

Many people claim that Bee Balm is so effective at drawing in hummingbirds that they no longer have to worry about filling their fur.
